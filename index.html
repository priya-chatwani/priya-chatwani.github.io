Priya Chatwani
Priya is an organizer and civic technologist.
I center anti-racism, anti-capitalism, anti-imperialism, and intersectionality in my efforts to redistribute resources and strengthen people-powered movements.

I'm building the solidarity economy.
As a worker-owner at Politics Rewired, I develop and share best practices for our company in collaboration with other worker-owned co-operatives.

I'm building community power.
I advocate for radical change and liberation in community with other South Asians, class-privilieged young people, abolitionists, and Los Angeles residents. In 2020, I created a mutual aid network that connected over 1000 people in LA County and was replicated in three other cities.

I'm building grassroots political power.
With Politics Rewired, I equip Leftist candidates and unions with organizing tools that save time, money, and effort. I also volunteer for campaigns like Marquita Bradshaw's Senate race and support voting rights groups with the Voter Empowerment Project.

I use she and they pronouns and grew up in Southern California on Tongva land. Catch me practicing yoga, concoting a new popcorn spice mix, or dreaming about coffee.
