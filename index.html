Priya Chatwani

Organizer. Technologist. Dancer.

I seek to reimagine and rebuild systems of care that support our collective liberation and allow everyone to thrive.

I'm Priya (she/they)! I grew up near Los Angeles and am usually on Ramaytush Ohlone land in the Bay Area, but I'm currently spending time in London. As a technologist and organizer, I center anti-capitalism, anti-imperialism, and intersectionality in my efforts to redestribute privilege and build political power on the Left.

Tech ~ I'm a worker-owner at Politics Rewired, a Leftist tech co-operative that builds organizing technology for electoral candidates, unions, and progressive organizations. Previously, I worked on public transit planning tools at Remix and interned at Twitter, Google, and Infosys. While at Stanford, I co-taught "Coding for Social Good" and worked with the Haas Center for Public Service to curate "public interest tech" panels, career newsletters, resource guides, and summer and post-graduate fellowships.

Organizing ~ Outside of my day job, I organize with Defund SFPD, Resource Generation, ASATA, and DSA SF, and I'm a founding board member of the Voter Empowerment Project. I've also volunteered as a Bluebonnet data fellow on Marquita Bradshaw’s landmark 2020 Senate campaign and as a youth leader for They See / Desi Blue, an organization mobilizing South Asian American voters.

Community ~ In March 2020, I helped build a mutual aid organization called LA Helping Hands for folks to deliver groceries and medications to their high-risk neighbors. Soon after, our friends started Helping Hands branches in their own cities, and as a Peace First ambassador, I helped support and fund other youth changemakers around the world. Other spaces where I find community include SF Adavu dance meet-ups, the Bay Area Solidary Summer youth organizer cohort, and Stanford's bhangra team and Center for South Asia.
