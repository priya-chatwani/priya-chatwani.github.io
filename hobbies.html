Dance

My introduction to dance began with Bharatanatyam, a South Indian dance form that I started learning in first grade. Throughout my childhood, I loved performing for others - whether it was a Bharatanatyam dance I had studied or a choreography of my own. In college, I joined the Stanford Bhangra team and helped us win our first competition award ever. Dance challenges me to learn new things and continue exploring different cultures and forms of expression.

Popcorn

I enjoy "spicing things up" and adding creative spice combinations to my popcorn. Here are some recommended popcorn and tea combinations:
  • Classic: Pepper, salt, and butter + Earl Grey tea
  • Masti Masala: Maggi Masala powder + Chai
  • Guata: Salt, lime, and Merkén - thank you to the Mapuche people for this amazing spice
  • Palomitas: Chili powder, cumin powder, paprika, Tajin, lime, Cotija cheese
  • Really Chili: Sriracha, salt, and sesame oil + Genmaicha green tea
  • It's Parmy Time: Lemon pepper, parmesan, and thyme
  • Spice Girls: Paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, and cajun seasoning
You can also mix your popcorn with other snacks like Takis, Doritos, Flamin Hot Cheetos, and Chex Mix. I also encourage you to experiment with different oils, such as coconut, olive, sesame, sunflower, grapeseed, and ghee.
